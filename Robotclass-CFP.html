Welcome to Robotics and Programming Class!

 

Welcome to our exciting after-school journey into the world of robotics and programming! This class is designed for young minds eager to explore the fascinating intersection of technology, creativity, and innovation. Our mission is to inspire and equip the next generation of thinkers, creators, and problem-solvers. We are thrilled to embark on this exciting adventure in robotics and programming with you. Let’s build and code our way to fun and discovery!

 

 

About the Class:

In this class, we will dive into the basics of robotics and computer programming. Students will learn how to design, build, and program robots using easy-to-understand, kid-friendly tools and languages. Our curriculum is tailored to encourage hands-on learning, creativity, and teamwork. Specifically, we will work with:

 

1. JIMU Robot Builderbots Kit: Students will tackle a 'Recycling Challenge', where they'll use the Builderbots Kit to create a robot capable of sorting different types of materials, learning about sensors and sorting algorithms in the process.

 

 

 

 

 

 

2. Airblock Modular Programmable Drone: The class will participate in a 'Robot Assemble' activity using the Airblock drone. Students will creatively reconfigure the modular pieces of the Airblock to form different shapes and designs, enabling it to navigate through various environments.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

3. DJI Tello Edu: In the 'Aerial Photography Project', students will script flight paths for the Tello to capture images from different altitudes and angles, introducing them to the basics of aerial imaging and remote sensing.

 

 

4. Turbo AI Truck: For the 'Automated Delivery Challenge', the Turbo AI Truck will be programmed to follow a path and make deliveries to specific locations on a mock-up map, demonstrating the use of AI in logistics and autonomous navigation.

 

 

 

 

Course Objectives:

·     Understand the Basics of Robotics: Learn the fundamental concepts of robotics, including mechanics, electronics, and automation.

·     Learn Programming Fundamentals: Get introduced to programming languages suitable for beginners, such as Scratch or Python, to control and give instructions to robots.

·     Develop Problem-Solving Skills: Engage in challenges and projects that foster critical thinking, creativity, and persistence.

·     Teamwork and Collaboration: Work in teams to build and program robots, encouraging collaboration and communication.

·     Fun and Interactive Learning: Experience learning through interactive projects, games, and competitions that make technology exciting and approachable.

 

 

Learning Outcomes:

Upon completion, students will:

·     Have a clear understanding of the components and construction of a basic robot.

·     Be able to program and troubleshoot simple robotic movements and tasks.

·     Possess a foundational grasp of how robots sense and respond to their environment.

·     Be prepared with the essential skills to pursue further education in robotics and programming.
